Bertin rebuilt from four numbers

Three constructions from the same tiny dataset: traffic-accident victims in France in 1958 by vehicle category. This version is rebuilt from scratch so the circle and cross examples follow the geometry of Bertin’s page more closely.

Data: pedestrians 28,951; bicycles 17,247; motorcycles 74,887; four-wheeled vehicles 63,071.

Method note. This is a historical/pedagogical reconstruction, not a current road-safety analysis. The four counts are enough to compare magnitudes; they are not enough to explain causes, risk, exposure, or change over time.
